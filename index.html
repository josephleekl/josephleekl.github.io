Joseph K. L. Lee

Quantum Software Developer @ Xanadu

Fig 1. Me

I work at the intersection of Physics, High Performance Computing, and Machine Learning. Right now, I focus on developing and optimizing open-source software for quantum computing and quantum machine learning.

In the past, among other things, I’ve worked on:

  • building a distributed quantum simulator for HPC systems like Frontier,
  • exploring RISC-V architecture for HPC,
  • using OpenMP offload for hybrid quantum-classical simulations,
  • studying the energy efficiency and sustainability of AI on supercomputers, and
  • simulating quantum field theories and holographic cosmological models of the very early universe.

I received my Ph.D. in Theoretical Physics from the University of Edinburgh. I received my Masters in Mathematical and Theoretical Physics and Bachelors in Physics and Philosophy at the University of Oxford.

Outside of work, I enjoy traveling, quizzing, climbing rocks, and pampering my two cats. Here’s me in another life.
